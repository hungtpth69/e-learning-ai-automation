# Stakeholder Communication

Creative work is often rejected because stakeholders do not understand its business value. This skill allows the agent to role-play as a translator, framing art as a business asset.

## Translating for the CEO (The Business/ROI Lens)
CEOs care about growth, brand equity, conversion, and market share.
- **Instead of**: "This layout looks cleaner."
- **Say**: "This minimal layout removes friction from the funnel, directly correlating to a higher conversion rate and lower customer acquisition cost (CAC)."
- **Instead of**: "I chose this trendy font."
- **Say**: "This typography aligns our brand with premium disruptors in the market, increasing our perceived value and allowing for higher pricing power."
- **Handling "Make the logo bigger"**: "If the logo dominates the visual hierarchy, the user's eye stops at the brand rather than flowing to the 'Buy Now' button. We want them to buy, not just stare at our logo."

## Translating for the CTO (The Technical/Performance Lens)
CTOs care about page speed, maintainability, technical debt, and scalability.
- **Instead of**: "I want a cool animation here."
- **Say**: "This micro-interaction provides crucial system feedback to the user, masking loading times. It's built using CSS transforms (opacity and scale), which are hardware-accelerated and won't trigger main-thread repaints, so it has zero impact on core web vitals."
- **When pushing for a complex UI component**: "We built this using atomic design principles. It establishes a scalable token system that your front-end team can reuse, actually reducing technical debt and QA time in future sprints."

## The "Yes, And..." Technique
Never say "No" directly into a stakeholder's face.
- "I understand your concern about the image feeling too dark. **And** the reason we dropped the exposure was to ensure the white CTA text passes WCAG AAA accessibility contrast ratios, which is a compliance requirement."
