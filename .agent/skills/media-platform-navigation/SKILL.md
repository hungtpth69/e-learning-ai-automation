# Cross-Platform Navigation

Different platforms have completely different algorithms, user behaviors, and content tagging systems. This skill guides the agent on how to tailor searches for specific platforms.

## Platform Search Strategies

### 1. Pinterest (Inspiration & Moodboards)
- **Best For**: Aesthetically driven research, mood boarding, color palettes, and stylistic references.
- **Search Tactics**: Use "aesthetic," "core," or "moodboard" suffixes. Look for specific Boards rather than just Pins to find curated collections.
- **Example**: `retro 90s ui aesthetic`

### 2. Behance & Dribbble (Professional Execution)
- **Best For**: High-fidelity UI/UX, typography, branding layouts, and professional vector illustrations.
- **Search Tactics**: Use UI/UX specific terminology (e.g., "onboarding flow," "bento box layout," "grid system"). Search within specific creative fields (e.g., Interaction Design).
- **Example**: `fintech dashboard bento UI layout`

### 3. Stock Repositories (Freepik, Envato, Unsplash, Getty)
- **Best For**: Raw, ready-to-use production assets (vectors, PNGs, isolated objects, photography).
- **Search Tactics**: Use extremely literal and descriptive tags. Include asset types and negative space requirements.
- **Example**: `isolated halftone dot pattern transparent background vector`

### 4. AI Generation Prompts (Midjourney, DALL-E, etc.)
- **Best For**: Creating entirely custom assets when stock cannot be found.
- **Search Tactics**: Combine medium, lighting, style, and subject matter into a concise prompt.
- **Example**: `macro shot of halftone dots, cyan and magenta, pop art style, studio lighting --v 6.0`

## Implementation Workflow

When asked to find an asset:
1. Identify the *type* of asset needed (inspiration, professional reference, or raw file).
2. Select the 1-2 most appropriate platforms.
3. Formulate the precise queries tailored to those specific platforms.
